Australian TV Energy Consumption

Televisions are one of the most commonly used household appliances in Australia. As screen sizes increase and new technologies emerge, understanding energy consumption has become increasingly important.

This website explores the energy efficiency of televisions currently available on the Australian market using data published by the Australian Government.

Why This Project Matters

Choosing an energy-efficient TV can help households reduce electricity costs and minimise environmental impact over time.

By analysing real-world product data, this project aims to highlight key patterns in TV screen size, screen technology and energy use that are relevant to everyday consumers.

About the Data

The data used in this project comes from the Australian Government Energy Rating database and includes information about registered TV models sold in Australia.

Key attributes include brand, screen size, screen technology, power consumption and energy star rating. The data was cleaned and processed using KNIME Analytics Platform before being visualised.

Television Energy Data Overview

This section presents insights derived from the Australian Government television energy rating dataset. The visualisations focus on common characteristics of TVs available in Australia and how these relate to energy consumption.

All charts shown below were generated after cleaning and processing the data using KNIME Analytics Platform.

TV Models by Brand

This chart shows the number of different television models offered by each brand in the Australian market.

Brands with a larger number of models typically have a stronger market presence and provide consumers with a wider range of choices.

The chart indicates that a small number of major brands dominate the market in terms of model variety, while many other brands offer only a limited selection of TVs.

Popular Screen Sizes

Screen size is an important factor for consumers, as it affects both viewing experience and energy consumption.

The chart below displays the frequency of different screen size categories available in Australia.

The data shows that 70+ inch and 60-69 inch televisions are the most commonly available sizes, suggesting a balance between screen size, cost and energy efficiency.

Screen Technology Availability

Television screen technology has a significant impact on both picture quality and power consumption.

This chart compares the availability of different screen technologies currently sold in Australia.

LCD-based technologies dominate the market, while OLED TVs are becoming increasingly popular. Plasma TVs appear very rarely, indicating that this technology is largely outdated.
